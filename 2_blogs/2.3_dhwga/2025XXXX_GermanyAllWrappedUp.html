Day 1 - Les Lindarets, Avoriaz, Morzine, and a little Chatel. Beautiful weather, some thick mud, sessioning the killer tech line at Morzine, and having constant sunshine to enjoy the incredible MTBing available and at my disposal. I got in as much riding as my body could handle, down the flow, the tech, the jumps, and the everything, and then bunked up at the nearby hostel for a little work and then into town for some good grub.













Day 2 - Les Crosets, Champery, and Chatel. Holy mother of god. What incredible trails. First Les Crosets, aka a dirt rollercoaster. Then Le Coupe du Monde in Champery, with mud so think that my bike couldn't spin its wheels anymore - I was litterally skiing on two wheels the majority of the way down and had to pull off my rear wheel to scrape off the dirt so it could spin freely again. Then Chatel to wrap and rip it up. Absolutely incredible riding day.

















Day 3 - Morgins. This place has hung in my mind for years and I was so unbelievably pumped to be back and riding it. Especially La Noir, the black technical trail. It was a quiet day at the park, but I ripped my heart out before having to head out midday for the trip home. But holy shit was the dirt perfect, the trails running great, and my multiple trips down La Noir made my brakes singe against the grass as I got to the bottom out of breath and riding an incredible high. No joke, the first time I came down La Noir was early in the day. I was beat, my hands tired, and out of breath, I got to the road and set my bike down, and my brake rotors hit the grass with a nice hiss from the heat of the rotors touching the dew on the grass. Fucking cool. But I ripped a great few lines and trails there, but ended on a rough note. Second to last run down La Noir and my front wheel slipped. I ended up burying my handlebar grip deep and the other end of my handlebar skewered me right in the thigh. I rode through it, but once I got home I noticed that I really got myself pretty good. Either way - Morgins might be one of my favorite places to ride in the Alps. Incredible, steep, feature-full, and just fucking incredible. But a little over a half-day in Morgins, and unfortunately I had to head back home. What a fucking incredible week of biking on some incredible trails!!!







Pre-Move Trip Home

Soon after my bike week in the Alps, it was off to another adventure. This time a work trip, but back to home. Timely too, as it was only a few months before I was slated to move back to the PNW, so I could utilize the time to get acquainted and start thinking about the blender I was throwing my life into in a move back to the USA.
